Maldives


The Maldives is a tropical paradise in the Indian Ocean. It is known for its crystal-clear waters, white-sand beaches, and colorful coral reefs. The Maldives is a popular honeymoon destination, but it is also a great place for families and adventure-seekers

  1. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - The Maldives is home to some of the best dive sites in the world.
  2. Island Hopping - There are over 1,000 islands in the Maldives, each with its own unique beauty.
  3. Water Sports - From surfing and windsurfing to kayaking and paddleboarding, there is no shortage of water sports in the Maldives.
  4. Male City - The capital city of the Maldives is a bustling hub of culture and history, with landmarks such as the Hukuru Miskiy mosque and the National Museum.
  5. Resort Life - Maldives has some of the world's most luxurious resorts, each with its own private island, white sand beach, and turquoise lagoon.
  • Mas Huni - A traditional Maldivian breakfast dish made with tuna, coconut, and chili.
  • Bis Keemiya - Deep-fried pastry filled with spiced fish, onions, and cabbage.
  • Rihaakuru - A thick fish paste that is used as a condiment in many Maldivian dishes.
  • Fihunu Mas - Grilled fish marinated in chili, garlic, and ginger.
  • Boshi Mashuni - A salad made with shredded unripe papaya, coconut, and chili.
